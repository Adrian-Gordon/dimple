Windy Gap
Text Item Title

John Meade Falkner (1858-1932) was a Wiltshire born novelist and poet who turned his talents to teaching and became tutor to the family of Sir Andrew Noble who managed one of the worlds largest armaments and engineering companies, Armstrong Whitworth & Company on Tyneside. Falkner became Chairman of the company in 1915 but on his retirement six years later moved away from Tyneside to take up the post of Honorary Librarian of Durham Cathedral. He and his wife made their home at Divinity House on Windy Gap where they redesigned part of the house to provide a bay window overlooking the river.

Falkner is best remembered for his childrens classic novel Moonfleet (1898), a tale of smuggling. But three years before his childrens classic, Falkner produced a short story incorporating many of the elements of traditional gothic literature. Horror, melodrama and atmosphere all blend together in The Lost Stradivarius (1895), a ghost story hinging on the theme of how evil can be manifested in the form of a single object, rather than in a human being. The object in this instance is a violin. In the book, an Oxford student, John Maltravers, plays ancient violin music which leads him to the discovery of a hidden cupboard in his rooms and thence along a dark path involving family love and destructive obsession.
